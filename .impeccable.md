## Design Context

### Users
Cuzdan Kocu is for Turkish families who want to understand spending, teach children money habits, and get proactive financial coaching without maintaining spreadsheets. The primary demo family is Ayse and Mehmet Yilmaz with their 12-year-old daughter Elif; a secondary individual-user path exists for young adults tracking first salary and credit-card debt.

### Brand Personality
Warm, practical, and quietly confident. The interface should feel like a calm family finance coach: helpful before it is asked, friendly without being childish, and never judgmental or authoritarian.

### Aesthetic Direction
Mobile-first household ledger rather than generic SaaS dashboard: ruled paper sheets, receipt tape, cash envelopes, stamped labels, deep ink text, kumbarasi/gold accents, and soft mint for reassurance. Motion should be purposeful and lightweight: one orchestrated page reveal, responsive hover/focus feedback, and gentle decorative motion that respects `prefers-reduced-motion`.

### Design Principles
1. Turkish copy is mandatory for every user-facing string.
2. Proactive guidance should be visually present even in empty or mock states.
3. Family context must be visible without exposing private data across roles.
4. Amounts and dates must feel local to Turkey: `1.250,50 ₺` and `gg.aa.yyyy`.
5. The UI should feel trustworthy and human, not banking-corporate or generic SaaS.
6. Avoid AI-dashboard tells: glow blobs, same-size card grids, icon-in-rounded-square sections, and generic hero/stat layouts.
